Major Human Joints (Plane, Saddle & Hinge)

About

1. The dynamic content demonstrate the major and important joints of human skeleton.
2. The learner can observe and learn the position(anatomy) and basic function(Physiology) of joints.
3. This activity will help learners to understand and retain the knowledge about the human skeletal system.

Instruction

1. Learner can see the three dimensional diagram of the human skeleton.
2. Learner can click on the flashcards with the name of major joints.
3. Once learner click on the particular card, they can see the detailed view and basic information of the joint.
4. Learners can swipe the flashcards to know about different joints.

Joints are like the nails, screws, and hinges that keep a house’s frame in place. A door hinge allows you to open and close a door on a limited axis. Now think about your knee’s movement. It moves in only one direction and stops when your leg is straight. This is because the joint allows only that much movement. Joints connect your bones and allow you to move; at the same time, joints determine which bone combinations will have motion and which will be stationary. This allows the skeleton to protect the body while also allowing for movement.
